First Aid Guide

Learn quick first aid techniques that can help save lives until professional medical assistance arrives.

Snake Bite

  • Stay calm and keep the victim still.
  • Immobilize the bitten limb below heart level.
  • Remove rings or tight clothing.
  • Call emergency medical services immediately.
  • Do NOT cut, suck, or apply ice to the wound.
⚠ Seek medical help immediately.

Burns

  • Cool the burn with running water for 20 minutes.
  • Remove jewelry before swelling begins.
  • Cover with a sterile dressing.
  • Do not apply butter, toothpaste, or oil.
⚠ Severe burns require immediate medical care.

Severe Bleeding

  • Apply firm pressure using a clean cloth.
  • Raise the injured limb if possible.
  • Do not remove blood-soaked bandages.
  • Add more dressing if necessary.
⚠ Heavy bleeding can become life-threatening.

Broken Bone

  • Do not move the injured limb.
  • Support it using a splint if trained.
  • Apply a cold pack to reduce swelling.
  • Seek emergency medical treatment.
⚠ Avoid attempting to straighten the bone.

Choking

  • Encourage the person to cough.
  • If unable to breathe, perform abdominal thrusts.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Begin CPR if the person becomes unconscious.
⚠ Act quickly—lack of oxygen is dangerous.

Heat Stroke

  • Move the person to a cool place.
  • Remove excess clothing.
  • Cool the body with wet towels or cold water.
  • Give water only if fully conscious.
⚠ Heat stroke is a medical emergency.

Animal Bite

  • Wash the wound with soap and water.
  • Apply antiseptic if available.
  • Cover with a clean bandage.
  • Visit a hospital for rabies evaluation if necessary.
⚠ Monitor for infection or unusual swelling.
This guide is for emergency first aid only. Always seek professional medical assistance whenever possible.
